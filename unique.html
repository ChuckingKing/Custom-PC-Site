Component Breakdown & Engineering Choices

GPU

GPU: We use the GTX 1070, a great option for solid 1080p gaming. It is powerful enough to handle modern titles at high settings while keeping the build affordable.

MB

Motherboard: This board was chosen for reliable power delivery, room for upgrades, and the features needed for a stable long-term system.

PSU

Power Supply: A trusted power supply keeps the rig safe. I avoid cheap units here because clean and consistent power matters for both longevity and system health.

CPU

CPU Cooler: The air cooler is selected for dependable cooling, low maintenance, and quiet operation so the system stays comfortable under load.

RAM

Memory: The system uses enough RAM for smooth daily use and gaming, with a configuration that leaves room to expand later if needed.

SSD

Storage: A fast SSD keeps boot times and game loads quick, while still giving enough capacity for a practical starter library.

CASE

Case: The case prioritizes airflow and smart cable routing, which helps the machine run cooler and look cleaner on the inside.

No Corners Cut Philosophy

When I build a rig, I build it well. That means focusing on high-quality components, good power, careful cable management, and airflow that protects your system for years to come. I am not just selling a PC, I am selling a setup I would feel good putting my name on.

Why I Am in a Strong Position to Build You a Good PC

  • I have personally built multiple gaming PCs and tuned them for stability and balance.
  • I focus on practical part choices instead of flashy upgrades that add cost without adding value.
  • I have real hands-on troubleshooting experience, from airflow issues to cable cleanup and hardware swaps.
  • I can walk through setup after the build, including basic software guidance and first-boot checks.
  • I care about the final user experience, not just whether the system powers on.
